Rotation Karakuri

Rotational Karakuri mechanisms rely on turning or spinning motion to transfer power or activate movement. This is typically done using gears, cams, or rotating shafts that interact with levers or pulleys.

These designs are widely used in factories for simple rotating tasks like opening lids or flipping trays. Their continuous movement potential, when driven manually, makes them efficient and user-friendly solutions.
