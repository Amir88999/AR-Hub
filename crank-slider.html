Crank Slider Karakuri

The crank-slider mechanism is a classic example of converting rotary motion into linear motion. It is often used in engines, pumps, and traditional automata. In Karakuri applications, it allows objects to be moved in straight lines using simple rotating input — a concept heavily rooted in mechanical tradition.

Its simplicity makes it ideal for manual or gravity-driven systems in lean factories. Crank-slider Karakuri helps automate motion like pushing, pulling, or lifting with no electricity involved — just physics and clever linkage.
