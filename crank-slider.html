Crank Slider Karakuri

The crank slider mechanism is a classic example of converting rotary motion into linear motion. Widely used in engines and mechanical devices, this mechanism uses a rotating crank attached to a slider that moves back and forth along a straight path.

In Karakuri applications, this mechanism is often used to animate dolls, open compartments, or perform repetitive tasks. It embodies simplicity and reliability, making it a favorite in both traditional and modern mechanical design.
