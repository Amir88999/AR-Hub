Crank Slider Karakuri

The Crank-Slider mechanism is one of the most commonly used mechanical linkages that converts rotary motion into linear motion. It consists of a rotating crank attached to a connecting rod which slides a piston or slider back and forth along a straight path. This simple yet powerful mechanism forms the basis of many engines and machines, especially in the automotive world.

In Karakuri-style automation, the crank-slider is celebrated for its rhythmic motion and reliable conversion of energy. It is often used to drive simple, cyclic tasks without requiring external energy input. Its historical use in Japanese wooden automata demonstrates how traditional mechanics can achieve precision and elegance with minimal complexity.
